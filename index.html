His original name was not Tutankhamun

Tutankhamun was originally named Tutankuten. This name, which literally means "living image of the Men", reflected the fact that Tutankuten's parents worshipped a sun god known as "the Aten". After a few years on the throne of the young king.

Egyptian astronomy begins in the Predynastic Period.

The death of a king had a strong connection to the stars for Ancient Egyptians. They believed once a king was deceased, their soul would rise to the heavens and become a star. Translated pyramid texts describe the king ascending and becoming the Morning Star among the Imperishable Stars of past kings.
